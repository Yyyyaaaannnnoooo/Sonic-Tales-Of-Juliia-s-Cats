## Juliia's Cats



The region of Juliiarie used to be very quiet and fertile, abundant in water. Sandwiched between the sea and the mountains, it offered refuge to many companion species. A plateau *(a picco sul mare)* was the perfect place for all the species to encounter and enjoy the space.

It was said that giant felines resided here. Three pools, curled in their distinctive shapes, stained the plateau.
The legend tells the story of these giant cats who, despite their natural aloofness, ultimately became protectors of the local water. This was because the water, which rendered the meadows fertile, created the perfect environment for all the species to thrive in quietness.

The meadow became so quiet that the cats fell into an eternal sleep, ultimately becoming the three lakes.

Centuries passed and the three cats continued napping. Until a hum began to curse the plateau. 

The hum in fact was the noise of tiny fans attached to millions of servers. Processing, analyzing and storing data. Beside the noise, the water of the lakes, the body of the cats was being stirred. Pumping fresh water to cool the data center, dumping in hot exhausted water after cooling silicon heat.

Suddenly the eternal sleep was broken.

Awaken to the loud hum the three cats found themselves in a chaotic mess. The meadow turning yellow, and all of the companion species slowly disappearing.

Saddened by this vision the cat reunited in council. 

How can we stop this hum?

their spirit form could not interact with earthly matter. Yet their voices could be heard. and so the council convened: let's purr until the data center shatters.

And so it started. Circling the infrastructure the three cats would purr the lowest they could. Tuning to the resonant frequencies of metal, quartz and silicon. The servers, the walls slowly and steadily started to vibrate, quartz clock to delay. server racks falling, and like a domino effect the whole hall of the data-centre became a pile of dead computation. 

And so the hum stopped.

And every time the data-centre would be rebuilt, the cat would purr it into piles of shattered servers.

And so the Juliiarie region was deemed unprofitable and unstable. Unworthy of investment. Ans so slowly and steadily the species came back repopulating the meadows. And the cats tired of their effort, fell again into the eternal sleep.

Then came the developers.

They saw the plateau not as sacred ground, but as the perfect site: elevated, cool, and seismically stable. They called the water "an efficient natural coolant." They called the silence "an absence of signal interference." They paved the meadow, and where the three pools once curled, they laid the foundations for a massive data center.

The hum began. A low, pervasive frequency thrummed through the bedrock, the sound of ten thousand servers processing, storing, transmitting. A vibration that had no natural counterpart in this quiet land. It was a sound without rest, without rhythm, a constant digital friction.

Deep in their aqueous slumber, the cats stirred. This new, relentless noise was an itch in the core of their being. Their dreamless sleep was invaded by the buzz of logic and light.
